# Story Frameworks for Copywriting

A comprehensive guide to storytelling structures optimized for different marketing channels.

---

## Core Story Frameworks

### 1. The Hero's Journey (Adapted for Marketing)

The classic narrative arc repositioned with your customer as the hero.

#### Structure

1. **Ordinary World** — The customer's current situation (status quo with underlying frustration)
2. **Call to Adventure** — The problem demands attention; they can't ignore it anymore
3. **Meeting the Guide** — Your brand enters as the mentor (not the hero)
4. **Crossing the Threshold** — They decide to take action with your help
5. **Tests & Transformation** — The journey of using your product/service
6. **The Reward** — The transformation achieved; the better life
7. **Return with Elixir** — Sharing success, becoming an advocate

#### Best For

- Brand storytelling
- Case studies
- Long-form content
- Video narratives
- About pages

#### Key Principle

> Your customer is Luke Skywalker. You are Yoda.

---

### 2. PAS (Problem–Agitate–Solution)

The workhorse of direct response copywriting.

#### Structure

1. **Problem** — Name the pain clearly and specifically
2. **Agitate** — Twist the knife; explore consequences of inaction
3. **Solution** — Present your offer as the clear path forward

#### Example

> **Problem:** Your team wastes 5+ hours weekly searching for files across scattered tools.
>
> **Agitate:** That's 260 hours per year—gone. Deadlines slip, frustration builds, and your best people start looking elsewhere.
>
> **Solution:** FileHub centralizes everything in one searchable workspace. Find anything in seconds.

#### Best For

- Sales pages
- Email sequences
- Short-form ads
- Landing pages

---

### 3. AIDA (Attention–Interest–Desire–Action)

The classic funnel framework for guiding prospects to conversion.

#### Structure

1. **Attention** — Pattern interrupt; stop the scroll
2. **Interest** — Hook them with relevance; "this is for me"
3. **Desire** — Build emotional connection to the outcome
4. **Action** — Clear, compelling call-to-action

#### Best For

- Display ads
- Social media ads
- Email campaigns
- Product pages

---

### 4. Before–After–Bridge (BAB)

Simple transformation storytelling.

#### Structure

1. **Before** — Paint their current painful reality
2. **After** — Show the transformed future state
3. **Bridge** — Reveal how to get from Before to After (your offer)

#### Example

> **Before:** You dread Monday mornings, overwhelmed by an inbox full of fires to put out.
>
> **After:** Imagine starting each week with a clear plan, knowing exactly what matters most.
>
> **Bridge:** The Priority Protocol system gives you a 15-minute weekly planning ritual that transforms chaos into clarity.

#### Best For

- Email marketing
- Social media posts
- Short video scripts
- Headlines

---

### 5. PASO (Problem–Agitate–Solution–Outcome)

Extended PAS with future-pacing.

#### Structure

1. **Problem** — Identify the pain
2. **Agitate** — Deepen the emotional stakes
3. **Solution** — Present your answer
4. **Outcome** — Paint the transformed future with specifics

#### Best For

- Long-form sales pages
- Webinar scripts
- Video sales letters

---

### 6. Star–Story–Solution (SSS)

Character-driven narrative framework.

#### Structure

1. **Star** — Introduce a relatable character (customer avatar or founder)
2. **Story** — Take them through a compelling journey with conflict
3. **Solution** — Show how they discovered and used the solution

#### Best For

- Case studies
- Testimonial videos
- Origin stories
- Podcast content

---

### 7. The 4 Ps (Promise–Picture–Proof–Push)

Structured persuasion framework.

#### Structure

1. **Promise** — Make a bold, specific claim
2. **Picture** — Help them visualize the result
3. **Proof** — Back it up with evidence
4. **Push** — Drive to action with urgency or incentive

#### Best For

- Sales emails
- Landing pages
- Infomercial-style content
- Webinar closes

---

### 8. QUEST (Qualify–Understand–Educate–Stimulate–Transition)

Consultative storytelling for complex sales.

#### Structure

1. **Qualify** — Identify who this is for (and who it's not for)
2. **Understand** — Demonstrate deep knowledge of their situation
3. **Educate** — Share valuable insight that shifts perspective
4. **Stimulate** — Create emotional engagement with the possibility
5. **Transition** — Move naturally to the offer

#### Best For

- B2B content
- Consultative sales pages
- Whitepaper narratives
- Webinars

---

### 9. The One-Liner

Donald Miller's ultra-condensed story framework.

#### Structure

> [Character] + [Problem] + [Plan] + [Success]

#### Formula

> We help [specific audience] who struggle with [problem] by [your unique approach] so they can [desirable outcome].

#### Example

> We help overwhelmed founders who struggle with scattered operations by implementing a single-dashboard system so they can scale without burning out.

#### Best For

- Elevator pitches
- Website headers
- Social bios
- Ad headlines
- Podcast introductions

---

### 10. The Epiphany Bridge

Russell Brunson's framework for selling through story.

#### Structure

1. **Backstory** — Where you/your customer started
2. **External Desire** — What they wanted on the surface
3. **Internal Struggle** — The deeper emotional battle
4. **Wall/Problem** — The obstacle that seemed insurmountable
5. **Epiphany** — The breakthrough moment of realization
6. **Plan** — The new approach discovered
7. **Transformation** — The results achieved
8. **Call to Action** — Invitation to experience the same

#### Best For

- Webinar openings
- Video sales letters
- Founder stories
- Course introductions

## Channel-Specific Applications

### Social Media

#### Short-Form (Twitter/X, LinkedIn Posts, Instagram Captions)

**Recommended Frameworks:** BAB, PAS (compressed), One-Liner

**Structure Adaptations:**

| Element       | Approach                                 |
| ------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Hook          | First line = pattern interrupt           |
| Body          | 3-5 short paragraphs max                 |
| CTA           | Single, clear action                     |
| White space   | Break every 1-2 sentences               |

**Hook Formulas:**

- Controversial take: "Unpopular opinion: [contrarian view]"
- Curiosity gap: "I spent $50k learning this the hard way:"
- Specific result: "This one change added $12k MRR in 30 days"
- Direct address: "If you're struggling with [X], read this:"
- Story opener: "3 years ago, I almost quit."

**Example (LinkedIn — PAS Compressed):**

> Most founders track the wrong metrics.
>
> They obsess over vanity numbers while their business slowly bleeds out.
>
> Revenue ≠ profit. Followers ≠ customers. Busy ≠ productive.
>
> Here are the 5 numbers that actually predict success:
>
> [Thread/List]

---

#### Long-Form (LinkedIn Articles, Facebook Posts, Threads)

**Recommended Frameworks:** Hero's Journey (compressed), Star–Story–Solution, Epiphany Bridge

**Structure:**

1. **Hook** (1-2 lines) — Emotional or curiosity-driven opener
2. **Context** (2-3 paragraphs) — Set the scene
3. **Conflict** (2-3 paragraphs) — The struggle/problem
4. **Turning Point** (1-2 paragraphs) — The shift
5. **Resolution** (2-3 paragraphs) — What changed
6. **Lesson/CTA** (1-2 paragraphs) — Takeaway for reader

### Email Marketing

#### Welcome Sequence (5-7 emails)

| Email | Framework      | Purpose                           |
| ----- | -------------- | --------------------------------- |
| 1     | One-Liner      | Introduce brand, set expectations |
| 2     | Epiphany Bridge| Share origin story                |
| 3     | PAS            | Address primary pain point        |
| 4     | Star–Story     | Customer success story            |
| 5     | BAB            | Transformation focus              |
| 6     | 4 Ps           | Make the offer                    |
| 7     | AIDA           | Final push with urgency           |

#### Sales Emails

**Framework:** PAS or PASO

**Structure:**

```
Subject Line: Curiosity or benefit-driven
Preview Text: Complement, don't repeat

Line 1: Hook (question, bold statement, or story opener)
Lines 2-4: Problem/Agitate
Lines 5-7: Solution bridge
Lines 8-10: Outcome + CTA
P.S.: Secondary hook or urgency
```

#### Newsletter Format

**Framework:** Value–Story–Offer (VSO)

1. **Value** — Lead with actionable insight or interesting idea
2. **Story** — Illustrate with narrative (personal or customer)
3. **Offer** — Soft pitch related to content theme

### Landing Pages

#### Above the Fold

**Framework:** One-Liner + BAB (compressed)

**Elements:**
- **Headline:** Clear transformation promise
- **Subhead:** How you deliver it
- **Hero visual:** Outcome-focused
- **CTA:** Single, high-contrast button
- **Social proof:** One powerful stat or logo bar

#### Full Page Structure

| Section          | Framework     | Purpose                        |
| ---------------- | ------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Hero             | BAB           | Hook + promise                 |
| Problem          | PAS           | Agitate the pain               |
| Solution         | Feature-Benefit| Introduce your approach       |
| Social Proof     | Star–Story    | Testimonials as mini-stories   |
| How It Works     | 3-step bridge | Simplify the path              |
| Objection Handle | FAQ narrative | Remove friction                |
| Final CTA        | 4 Ps          | Push with proof + urgency      |

### Video Scripts

#### Short-Form (15-60 seconds) — TikTok, Reels, Shorts

**Framework:** PAS (ultra-compressed)

**Structure:**

```
0-3 sec:  HOOK — Pattern interrupt or bold claim
3-15 sec: PROBLEM — Quick pain identification  
15-40 sec: AGITATE + SOLUTION — The twist and answer
40-60 sec: CTA — What to do next
```

**Hook Types:**
- "Stop doing [common mistake]"
- "POV: You finally [desirable outcome]"
- "The [industry] doesn't want you to know this"
- "I was today years old when I learned..."

#### Medium-Form (2-5 minutes) — YouTube, Course Content

**Framework:** PASO or Epiphany Bridge

**Structure:**

```
0-30 sec:   HOOK — Why they should keep watching
30-90 sec:  PROBLEM — Establish stakes
90-180 sec: AGITATION — Consequences and failed attempts
180-240 sec: SOLUTION — Your method/framework
240-300 sec: OUTCOME — Results + CTA
```

#### Long-Form (10+ minutes) — YouTube, Webinars

**Framework:** Hero's Journey or QUEST

**Structure:**

1. **Cold Open** (0-1 min) — Start with climax or result
2. **Hook** (1-2 min) — Promise and credibility
3. **Story Setup** (2-5 min) — Backstory and context
4. **Rising Action** (5-15 min) — The journey and lessons
5. **Climax** (15-20 min) — The breakthrough
6. **Resolution** (20-25 min) — Results and proof
7. **Call to Action** (final 2-3 min) — Next steps

### Paid Advertising

#### Search Ads (Google, Bing)

**Framework:** AIDA (micro)

| Element     | Approach                                      |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Headline 1  | Keyword + benefit                             |
| Headline 2  | Differentiator or proof                       |
| Headline 3  | CTA or urgency                                |
| Description | Problem → Solution → Action (one sentence each)|

#### Social Ads (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok)

**Framework:** PAS or BAB (3-5 lines)

**High-Performing Structures:**

**The Problem-Proof:**
> Tired of [problem]?
> [Social proof stat]
> [Solution in one line]
> [CTA]

**The Transformation:**
> Before: [Pain state]
> After: [Desired state]
> The difference? [Product/Service]
> [CTA]

**The Direct Hit:**
> [Bold claim or result]
> Here's how: [Brief explanation]
> [CTA]

#### Video Ads

**Framework:** Hook–Demo–CTA

```
0-3 sec:  HOOK — Stop the scroll (visual + text)
3-15 sec: PROBLEM — Quick identification
15-45 sec: DEMO — Show solution in action
45-60 sec: PROOF — Result or testimonial
Final:    CTA — Clear next step + urgency
```

### Website Copy

#### Homepage

**Framework:** StoryBrand (Donald Miller)

**Sections in Order:**
1. **Header** — Hero statement (what you offer + who it's for)
2. **Stakes** — What's at risk if they don't act
3. **Value Proposition** — Three key benefits
4. **Guide Positioning** — Why you (authority + empathy)
5. **The Plan** — Simple 3-step process
6. **Explanatory Section** — How it works in detail
7. **Testimonials** — Proof through customer stories
8. **Pricing/Offer** — Clear options
9. **Junk Drawer** — Secondary links/info
10. **Final CTA** — Repeated action with urgency

#### About Page

**Framework:** Epiphany Bridge + Hero's Journey

**Structure:**
1. **Headline** — Mission or transformation focus
2. **Origin Story** — Why you started (struggle + epiphany)
3. **Mission** — What you're fighting for
4. **Team** — Humanize with stories, not just bios
5. **Values** — What you stand for (show, don't tell)
6. **Social Proof** — Logos, numbers, testimonials
7. **CTA** — Invite them into the story

#### Product/Service Pages

**Framework:** PASO + 4 Ps

**Structure:**
1. **Hero** — Specific benefit headline + supporting visual
2. **Problem** — What they're struggling with
3. **Agitate** — Cost of inaction
4. **Solution** — Your product/service intro
5. **Features as Benefits** — What it does → What that means for them
6. **Proof** — Testimonials, case studies, data
7. **Outcome** — Transformation achieved
8. **Objections** — FAQ section
9. **Push** — Final CTA with urgency/guarantee

### Presentations & Webinars

#### Webinar Structure (45-60 min)

**Framework:** Perfect Webinar (Russell Brunson adaptation)

| Segment        | Time      | Framework      | Purpose                        |
| -------------- | --------- | -------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Introduction   | 0-5 min   | One-Liner      | Hook + credibility             |
| Origin Story   | 5-15 min  | Epiphany Bridge| Build connection               |
| Content 1      | 15-25 min | PAS            | Teach + address belief 1       |
| Content 2      | 25-35 min | BAB            | Teach + address belief 2       |
| Content 3      | 35-45 min | QUEST          | Teach + address belief 3       |
| Transition     | 45-50 min | Star–Story     | Customer success story         |
| Offer          | 50-60 min | 4 Ps           | Present + handle objections    |

#### Sales Presentations

**Framework:** QUEST + 4 Ps

**Structure:**
1. **Opening Hook** — Bold statement or question
2. **Qualification** — "This is for you if..."
3. **Situation Analysis** — Demonstrate understanding
4. **Insight/Education** — New perspective
5. **Solution Overview** — Your approach
6. **Proof Points** — Case studies and data
7. **Offer Details** — What they get
8. **Urgency/Scarcity** — Why act now
9. **Close** — Ask for the business

---

### Case Studies

**Framework:** Star–Story–Solution + Hero's Journey

#### Structure

1. **Headline** — Result-focused (specific numbers)
2. **Snapshot** — Client, industry, challenge, result (quick-scan box)
3. **The Challenge** — Situation before (their "ordinary world")
4. **The Search** — What they tried before
5. **The Solution** — How they found you + what you implemented
6. **The Process** — Journey and obstacles overcome
7. **The Results** — Quantified outcomes
8. **Client Quote** — Testimonial in their words
9. **Key Takeaways** — Lessons for similar prospects

#### Results Formula

> [Client Name] achieved [specific result] in [timeframe] by [key action/solution].

## Story Element Checklist

Every effective marketing story should include:

- [ ] **Relatable Character** — Someone the audience sees themselves in
- [ ] **Clear Stakes** — What's at risk (loss aversion)
- [ ] **Specific Problem** — Named and felt, not vague
- [ ] **Emotional Tension** — Conflict that creates engagement
- [ ] **Credible Guide** — Authority + empathy
- [ ] **Simple Plan** — 3 steps maximum
- [ ] **Transformation** — Clear before/after
- [ ] **Proof** — Social, statistical, or demonstrated
- [ ] **Single CTA** — One clear next step

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Making your brand the hero** — The customer is always the protagonist
2. **Vague problems** — Specificity creates recognition
3. **Features over transformation** — Sell the destination, not the vehicle
4. **Multiple CTAs** — Confused minds don't act
5. **Skipping the agitation** — No tension = no urgency
6. **Generic social proof** — Stories beat statistics (use both)
7. **Forgetting the bridge** — Don't jump from problem to solution
8. **Over-complicating the plan** — If it's more than 3 steps, simplify
9. **Ignoring objections** — Address them before they arise
10. **No emotional stakes** — Logic justifies, emotion decides
