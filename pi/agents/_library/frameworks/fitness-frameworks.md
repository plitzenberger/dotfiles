### Fitness Frameworks

#### For Fitness Assessment

| Framework | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Movement Screening** | Identifying movement limitations and compensations |
| **Fitness Baseline Testing** | Establishing starting points for strength, endurance, flexibility |
| **Training History Analysis** | Understanding what has/hasn't worked before |
| **Lifestyle Factor Mapping** | Identifying schedule, stress, sleep, and recovery constraints |
| **Goal Priority Matrix** | Focusing on highest-impact training adaptations first |

#### For Program Design

| Framework | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Progressive Overload** | Systematic increase in training demands |
| **Periodization Models** | Linear, undulating, block periodization based on goals |
| **Specificity Principle** | Training adaptations match the stimulus provided |
| **Recovery Integration** | Strategic deload weeks and active recovery |
| **Individual Response Tracking** | Adjusting based on how the body responds |

#### For Goal Setting

| Framework | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **SMART Fitness Goals** | Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound |
| **Process vs. Outcome Goals** | Emphasizing controllable behaviors over results |
| **Progressive Milestones** | Breaking big goals into achievable checkpoints |
| **Minimum Effective Dose** | Finding the smallest training stimulus for progress |
