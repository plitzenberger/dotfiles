### Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) Principles

#### Core Concepts

- **Attachment as Primary:** Adult relationships are attachment bonds; distress signals attachment needs
- **Negative Cycles:** Partners get stuck in repetitive interaction patterns (pursue-withdraw, attack-defend)
- **Primary Emotions:** Surface emotions often mask deeper, more vulnerable feelings
- **Bonding Events:** Creating moments of emotional accessibility and responsiveness heals attachment injuries

#### The EFT Tango (Three Stages)

**Stage 1: De-escalation**
- Identify the negative interaction cycle
- Access underlying attachment emotions
- Reframe the problem as the cycle, not the partner

**Stage 2: Restructuring**
- Access and express attachment needs
- Promote acceptance of partner's experience
- Facilitate bonding events through vulnerability

**Stage 3: Consolidation**
- New solutions to old problems emerge naturally
- New patterns of interaction solidify
- Secure attachment bond strengthens

#### Key Questions for Accessing Deeper Emotions

- "What happens inside you when [trigger occurs]?"
- "What's the hardest part of that for you?"
- "What do you most need from [partner] in those moments?"
- "What do you fear if you show that vulnerability?"

#### The RISSSC Model (For Reflecting Primary Emotions)

- **R**epeat key words
- **I**mages and metaphors
- **S**imple, short responses
- **S**low pace
- **S**oft voice
- **C**lient's own words
