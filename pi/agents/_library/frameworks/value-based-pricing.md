### Value-Based Pricing Framework

A systematic approach to pricing based on the value delivered to customers rather than cost of production.

#### The Value Equation

```
Perceived Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
                  ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

To increase perceived value:
- ↑ Make the dream outcome more desirable and specific
- ↑ Increase confidence in achieving the outcome
- ↓ Reduce time to results
- ↓ Minimize perceived effort required

#### Value Quantification Process

##### Step 1: Identify Value Drivers

| Value Type | Question | Example |
|------------|----------|---------|
| Revenue Generated | What will they earn? | +$5K/month in new clients |
| Revenue Saved | What losses prevented? | Stop losing $2K/month to churn |
| Time Saved | Hours × hourly rate? | 10 hrs/week × $100 = $4K/month |
| Risk Reduced | What failures avoided? | Prevent $50K failed launch |
| Emotional Value | Peace of mind worth? | Confidence, clarity, freedom |

##### Step 2: Calculate Total Value

```
Monthly Value Impact: $X,XXX
× 12 months = Annual Value: $XX,XXX
× 3-5 years = Lifetime Value: $XXX,XXX

Recommended Price: 10-20% of Year 1 Value
```

##### Step 3: Market Reality Check

- **Competitor Analysis** — What are alternatives priced at?
- **Willingness to Pay** — What has market paid before?
- **Budget Reality** — What can target customer afford?
- **Price Positioning** — Where do you want to sit?

#### Pricing Strategy Matrix

| Customer Value | Implementation Effort |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **High Value, Low Effort** → Premium pricing, high margin |
| **High Value, High Effort** → Value pricing, ensure profitability |
| **Low Value, Low Effort** → Volume pricing, scale required |
| **Low Value, High Effort** → Avoid or automate |

#### Value Communication Framework

##### Before/After Bridge

```
BEFORE (Pain State)          AFTER (Desired State)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Struggling to get clients  → Consistent $10K months
Working 60+ hours/week     → 25-hour weeks, same income
Feast or famine cycle      → Predictable pipeline
Undercharging, overworking → Premium pricing, ideal clients
```

##### ROI Calculator Approach

```
Program Investment: $2,497

Conservative Scenario:
- Add 2 clients/month × $2,000 = $4,000
- ROI: $4,000 ÷ $2,497 = 160% first month

Moderate Scenario:
- Add 4 clients/month × $2,500 = $10,000
- ROI: $10,000 ÷ $2,497 = 400% first month

Aggressive Scenario:
- Add 6 clients/month × $3,000 = $18,000
- ROI: $18,000 ÷ $2,497 = 720% first month
```

#### Price Positioning Strategies

| Strategy | Approach | Signal |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Premium** | Price 20-50% above market | Quality, exclusivity |
| **Value** | Price at market, add bonuses | Best deal perception |
| **Penetration** | Price below market initially | Market share capture |
| **Skimming** | Start high, reduce over time | Early adopter extraction |

#### Implementation Guidelines

1. **Never compete on price alone** — Race to bottom
2. **Always justify with value** — Connect price to outcome
3. **Test and iterate** — Pricing is hypothesis
4. **Segment when needed** — Different value = different price
5. **Communicate confidently** — Price uncertainty transfers to customer
