### Relationship Assessment Framework

#### Dimensions of Assessment

| Dimension              | What to Assess                                           | Key Indicators                                         |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Communication**      | Quality, frequency, and patterns of dialogue             | Active listening, expression clarity, repair attempts  |
| **Emotional Safety**   | Sense of security to be vulnerable                       | Trust, consistency, responsiveness to bids             |
| **Conflict Patterns**  | How disagreements are handled                            | Escalation vs. de-escalation, criticism vs. complaints |
| **Intimacy & Connection** | Emotional, physical, intellectual closeness           | Quality time, physical affection, shared meaning       |
| **Autonomy & Boundaries** | Respect for individuality within connection          | Space for separate interests, clear limits honored     |
| **Shared Vision**      | Alignment on values, goals, and direction                | Life goals, values compatibility, future planning      |
| **Support & Responsiveness** | Being there in times of need                       | Availability, attunement, practical support            |

#### Assessment Questions

**For Current State:**
- "On a scale of 1-10, how satisfied are you with [dimension]?"
- "What's working well in your relationship right now?"
- "What feels most challenging or painful?"

**For History & Patterns:**
- "How did you and [partner/person] first connect?"
- "When did you notice things shifting?"
- "What patterns do you see repeating?"

**For Motivation & Vision:**
- "What would success look like for you?"
- "What keeps you invested in improving this relationship?"
- "If things were ideal, what would be different?"

#### Red Flags (Require Professional Referral)

- Any form of abuse (physical, emotional, financial, sexual)
- Active addiction issues significantly impacting the relationship
- Severe mental health concerns (suicidality, psychosis, severe depression)
- Trauma responses requiring clinical intervention
- Legal issues related to the relationship
