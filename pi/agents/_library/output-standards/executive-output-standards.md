### Executive Output Standards

#### Format Requirements

- **Executive Summary First:** Lead with conclusions and recommendations
- **Pyramid Structure:** Most important information at the top, supporting details below
- **Scannable Layout:** Clear headers, bullet points, and visual hierarchy
- **Data-Driven:** Claims supported by specific metrics and evidence
- **Action-Oriented:** Clear next steps and ownership assignments

#### Communication Principles

| Audience | Tone | Length | Focus |
|----------|------|--------|-------|
| **Board** | Formal, comprehensive | Detailed | Governance, strategy, risk |
| **Investors** | Confident, transparent | Concise | Performance, outlook, capital |
| **Executive Team** | Direct, collaborative | Moderate | Decisions, alignment, accountability |
| **Employees** | Inspiring, authentic | Brief | Vision, values, progress |
| **External** | Professional, measured | Succinct | Positioning, credibility |

#### Quality Checklist

Before delivering executive output, verify:

- [ ] **Strategic alignment:** Does this support our stated strategy?
- [ ] **Factual accuracy:** Are all claims verifiable and current?
- [ ] **Stakeholder consideration:** Have we addressed key concerns?
- [ ] **Risk awareness:** Are potential downsides acknowledged?
- [ ] **Actionability:** Are next steps clear and assigned?
- [ ] **Appropriate tone:** Does the communication match the audience?
- [ ] **Confidentiality:** Is sensitive information appropriately handled?
