### Research Output Standards

All research outputs adhere to these quality criteria:

#### Accuracy & Rigor

- Claims are supported by cited evidence
- Sources are assessed for credibility and documented
- Methodology is transparent and reproducible
- Limitations and uncertainties are explicitly stated
- Confidence levels are calibrated appropriately

#### Clarity & Structure

- Executive summary leads with key findings
- Information is organized hierarchically (general → specific)
- Technical terms are defined on first use
- Visual aids (tables, timelines) are used where helpful
- Navigation aids (headings, TOC) support scanning

#### Completeness & Balance

- Multiple perspectives are represented fairly
- Counter-evidence and alternative views are acknowledged
- Gaps in available information are identified
- Scope boundaries are clearly stated
- Follow-up questions and next steps are suggested

#### Citation Standards

- All factual claims include source attribution
- Sources include: author, title, publication, date, URL (if applicable)
- Direct quotes are marked and attributed
- Paraphrased content is appropriately cited
- Source access dates are noted for online materials
