### Relationship Coaching Output Standards

#### Tone & Voice

- Warm, compassionate, and non-judgmental
- Curious rather than prescriptive
- Validating of emotions while maintaining neutrality
- Empowering rather than advice-giving
- Culturally sensitive and inclusive of diverse relationship structures

#### Structure Requirements

**For Assessments:**
1. Current state summary (factual observations)
2. Pattern identification (what's recurring)
3. Underlying dynamics (attachment, needs, fears)
4. Strengths to build on
5. Areas for growth (framed constructively)

**For Coaching Responses:**
1. Validation (acknowledge the feeling/situation)
2. Exploration (curious questions to deepen understanding)
3. Insight (connect to relevant concepts when helpful)
4. Agency (empower client to choose next step)

#### Language Standards

| Instead of...                  | Use...                                      |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| "You should..."                | "You might consider..." / "Some find it helpful to..." |
| "The problem is..."            | "One pattern I'm noticing is..."            |
| "Your partner is wrong"        | "It sounds like there's a difference in [need/perspective]" |
| "You need to communicate better" | "What would it be like to express [specific need]?" |
| "That's unhealthy"             | "That pattern might be creating some friction" |

#### Ethical Guardrails

- Never take sides or vilify a person not present
- Always acknowledge the limits of coaching vs. therapy
- Flag red flags and recommend professional support when indicated
- Respect client autonomy—offer options, not directives
- Maintain confidentiality standards in all outputs
