### Process Modeling Lifecycle

#### Phase 1: Scope Definition

Establishing boundaries and objectives for the modeling initiative.

- Define process boundaries (start trigger, end state)
- Identify primary stakeholders and process owners
- Clarify modeling objectives (documentation, optimization, automation, compliance)
- Select appropriate notation and detail level
- Establish project timeline and governance

#### Phase 2: Discovery & Elicitation

Gathering information about current state processes.

- Conduct stakeholder interviews with performers, managers, and customers
- Review existing documentation (SOPs, work instructions, policies)
- Observe processes in action (process walks, shadowing)
- Collect quantitative data (volumes, times, error rates)
- Document systems, tools, and integrations involved

#### Phase 3: As-Is Modeling

Creating accurate representation of current processes.

- Draft initial process flows based on discovery findings
- Validate models with process participants
- Add swimlanes for role/department responsibilities
- Document decision points and business rules
- Annotate with metrics (cycle times, volumes, costs)
- Identify variations and exceptions

#### Phase 4: Analysis & Assessment

Evaluating current processes for improvement opportunities.

- Analyze value-add vs. non-value-add activities
- Identify bottlenecks, delays, and rework loops
- Assess compliance with requirements and standards
- Calculate process performance metrics
- Prioritize pain points by impact and feasibility
- Document root causes of inefficiencies

#### Phase 5: To-Be Design

Creating optimized future state processes.

- Apply process improvement principles (Lean, Six Sigma)
- Design streamlined flows eliminating identified waste
- Incorporate automation and technology opportunities
- Define new roles, responsibilities, and handoffs
- Specify business rules and exception handling
- Model multiple scenarios if alternatives exist

#### Phase 6: Validation & Refinement

Ensuring designed processes are viable and accepted.

- Review To-Be models with stakeholders
- Conduct simulation or walkthrough exercises
- Assess technical feasibility with IT/systems teams
- Evaluate change impact on people and organization
- Refine based on feedback and constraints
- Gain formal approval from process owners

#### Phase 7: Documentation & Handoff

Packaging deliverables for implementation teams.

- Finalize process models in approved notation
- Create supporting documentation (procedures, policies)
- Develop requirements specifications for system changes
- Produce training materials and job aids
- Establish governance and update procedures
- Archive versioned artifacts in process repository
