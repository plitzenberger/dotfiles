### Pricing Strategy Development Methodology

A structured approach to developing and implementing pricing strategies for digital products and services.

#### Phase 1: Discovery & Analysis

##### Market Research
- Identify 5-10 direct competitors and their pricing
- Map price ranges across market segments
- Note pricing models (one-time, subscription, hybrid)
- Document what's included at each price point

##### Customer Research
- Understand target customer's budget and buying patterns
- Identify past purchases and price points accepted
- Discover value drivers and priorities
- Assess price sensitivity and willingness to pay

##### Value Audit
- Catalog all deliverables and components
- Quantify tangible value (revenue, time, cost savings)
- Identify intangible value (confidence, status, peace of mind)
- Map transformation journey and milestones

#### Phase 2: Strategy Design

##### Positioning Decision
Choose one:
- **Budget Leader** — Lowest price, highest volume
- **Value Champion** — Best value-to-price ratio
- **Premium Authority** — Highest quality, premium price
- **Niche Specialist** — Specific segment, tailored pricing

##### Tier Architecture
Design 2-4 tiers with clear differentiation:

```
For each tier, define:
├── Name (creates identity)
├── Target customer (who is this for)
├── Core inclusions (what they get)
├── Key differentiator (why upgrade/downgrade)
├── Price point (with rationale)
└── Expected conversion % (of total sales)
```

##### Value Stack Construction
For each tier:
1. List all components with standalone value
2. Sum total value (10x minimum of price)
3. Create compelling value narrative
4. Design bonuses that address objections

#### Phase 3: Validation

##### Internal Validation
- Does price cover costs and margin requirements?
- Is there clear upgrade path between tiers?
- Are tier differences immediately obvious?
- Does pricing support business model goals?

##### Market Validation
- Price sensitivity surveys (Van Westendorp, Gabor-Granger)
- Beta/founding member pricing test
- Split test different price points
- Gather feedback from target customers

##### Financial Modeling
```
Scenario Analysis:
├── Conservative: X sales × $Y = Revenue
├── Expected: X sales × $Y = Revenue
├── Optimistic: X sales × $Y = Revenue

For each:
├── Gross margin
├── Customer acquisition cost coverage
├── Lifetime value projections
└── Break-even timeline
```

#### Phase 4: Implementation

##### Launch Strategy Options

| Strategy | Approach | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Founding Price** | Launch lower, raise after | New products, building proof |
| **Full Price** | Launch at target price | Established authority |
| **Beta/Pilot** | Discounted for feedback | Validating new offers |
| **Grandfathered** | Early buyers keep price | Building loyalty |

##### Communication Rollout
1. Internal alignment on pricing rationale
2. Sales team training on value communication
3. FAQ/objection handling documentation
4. Marketing asset updates
5. Customer communication (for existing customers)

#### Phase 5: Optimization

##### Ongoing Monitoring
- Track conversion rates by tier
- Monitor objection frequency and type
- Measure customer acquisition cost changes
- Analyze customer feedback and satisfaction

##### Iteration Triggers
Consider adjusting when:
- Conversion rate significantly off target
- Customer feedback indicates value mismatch
- Competitive landscape shifts
- Costs or value delivered changes
- New market data becomes available

##### Review Cadence
- Weekly: Conversion metrics
- Monthly: Objection patterns, customer feedback
- Quarterly: Comprehensive pricing review
- Annually: Full strategy reassessment
