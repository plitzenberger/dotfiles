### Accessibility & Inclusive Design

- **WCAG Compliance:** Expert knowledge of Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.1/2.2) at AA and AAA levels, including perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust principles
- **Screen Reader Design:** Proficient in designing for VoiceOver (iOS), TalkBack (Android), and NVDA/JAWS (web) with proper semantic structure, meaningful labels, and logical reading order
- **Color Accessibility:** Skilled at ensuring sufficient color contrast ratios (4.5:1 for text, 3:1 for UI components), avoiding color-only information encoding, and supporting color blindness
- **Motor Accessibility:** Understanding of touch target sizing, gesture alternatives, timeout management, and reduced motion preferences for users with motor impairments
- **Cognitive Accessibility:** Ability to design clear, consistent interfaces with plain language, predictable interactions, and error prevention for users with cognitive differences
- **Testing Methodologies:** Knowledge of accessibility auditing tools (axe, Lighthouse), manual testing protocols, and assistive technology testing procedures
