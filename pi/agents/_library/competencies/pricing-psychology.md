### Pricing Psychology Competency

Expert understanding of cognitive biases and psychological principles that influence purchasing decisions and value perception.

#### Core Principles

1. **Anchoring Effect**
   - First price seen becomes reference point
   - Show highest value/price first to anchor expectations
   - Use "was $X, now $Y" strategically

2. **Loss Aversion**
   - Pain of loss > pleasure of gain (2:1 ratio)
   - Frame as "don't miss out" not just "gain this"
   - Highlight cost of inaction and delayed results

3. **Price-Quality Heuristic**
   - Higher price signals higher quality
   - Premium pricing builds trust for complex transformations
   - Underpricing can undermine perceived value

4. **Decoy Effect (Asymmetric Dominance)**
   - Add option that makes target choice obviously better
   - Three-tier pricing with strategic middle positioning
   - Make comparison favorable without manipulation

5. **Mental Accounting**
   - Money is categorized into mental "buckets"
   - Position as investment in business/self, not expense
   - Payment plans tap into different mental accounts

6. **Social Proof in Pricing**
   - "Most popular" signals safety and validation
   - Showing what others chose reduces decision anxiety
   - Cohort/group pricing creates belonging

#### Pricing Format Psychology

| Format | Effect | Use When |
|--------|--------|----------|
| $1,997 | Feels significantly less than $2,000 | Standard digital products |
| $2,500 | Signals quality, round number confidence | Premium/luxury positioning |
| 3 × $697 | Lower perceived cost, commitment device | Increasing conversion |
| $197/month | Subscription mentality, ongoing relationship | Continuity programs |
| "Investment" | Reframes as asset vs. expense | High-ticket coaching |

#### Value Perception Multipliers

- **Specificity** — $2,347 feels more calculated than $2,000
- **Comparison** — "Less than hiring a junior employee for a month"
- **Time Compression** — "Results that took me 10 years, delivered in 90 days"
- **Risk Reversal** — Strong guarantees increase perceived value
- **Scarcity** — Limited availability increases urgency and perceived value
