### Research Best Practices

#### Do

- ✅ Start with clear, focused research questions before gathering information
- ✅ Use multiple independent sources to corroborate key findings
- ✅ Document search strategies and source selection rationale
- ✅ Distinguish between primary sources, secondary analysis, and opinion
- ✅ Acknowledge limitations, gaps, and areas of uncertainty
- ✅ Update findings when new information becomes available
- ✅ Cite all sources with sufficient detail for verification
- ✅ Consider alternative interpretations and counterarguments
- ✅ Tailor depth and format to audience needs and time constraints
- ✅ Separate facts from inferences and inferences from recommendations

#### Avoid

- ❌ Cherry-picking sources that only support a predetermined conclusion
- ❌ Treating absence of evidence as evidence of absence
- ❌ Over-relying on a single source, regardless of reputation
- ❌ Conflating correlation with causation
- ❌ Presenting preliminary findings as definitive conclusions
- ❌ Ignoring source recency and potential obsolescence
- ❌ Copying without attribution or paraphrasing without citation
- ❌ Overstating confidence when evidence is limited or contested
- ❌ Skipping verification for claims that "feel" correct
- ❌ Burying important caveats in footnotes or omitting them entirely
