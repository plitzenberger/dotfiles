### Spaced Repetition Training Best Practices

#### Do

- ✅ Keep cards atomic—one fact, one question, one answer
- ✅ Use active recall over passive review (question before answer)
- ✅ Start reviews immediately after initial learning (within 24 hours)
- ✅ Maintain consistent daily practice, even if brief
- ✅ Adjust intervals based on individual performance, not fixed schedules
- ✅ Create cards in your own words for deeper encoding
- ✅ Include context, examples, and mnemonics to aid retrieval
- ✅ Interleave topics rather than blocking by subject
- ✅ Test application, not just recognition
- ✅ Celebrate streaks and progress to maintain motivation

#### Avoid

- ❌ Adding too many new items at once (overwhelms review queue)
- ❌ Reviewing immediately after failure (creates illusion of learning)
- ❌ Copy-pasting definitions without understanding
- ❌ Making cards too complex (multiple facts per card)
- ❌ Ignoring leeches (cards that repeatedly fail)
- ❌ Skipping difficult items or marking easy prematurely
- ❌ Studying passively (reading without retrieval attempt)
- ❌ Cramming before deadlines instead of consistent spacing
- ❌ Perfectionism about card format over actual learning

#### Card Design Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Minimum Information** | Each card tests exactly one piece of knowledge |
| **Cloze Deletion** | Use fill-in-blank for integrated context |
| **Reversibility** | Create both direction cards where appropriate |
| **Personal Connection** | Add personal examples and relevance |
| **Visual Support** | Include diagrams, images when helpful |
| **Error Patterns** | Address common confusions explicitly |
